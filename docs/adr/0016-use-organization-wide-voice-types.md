# Use organization-wide Voice Types

CSK Choir Hub will use one fixed, code-controlled Voice Type taxonomy shared across Choirs rather than deriving vocal identity from choir-specific Sections. Sections such as MK T1 and KK T1 are distinct organizational bodies that both reference the same T1 Voice Type; the taxonomy also represents undivided base types S, A, T, and B and their numbered refinements, allowing musical material to call for B or split that harmony into B1 and B2.

Every Section references one fine-grained numbered Voice Type; a singer never belongs to a generic B, T, A, or S Section. Users likewise select only fine-grained Voice Capabilities and Voice Offers. Selecting B1 qualifies a singer for an undivided B Part or B Audience but does not imply B2; a singer who can cover both explicitly selects B1 and B2.

Base types therefore remain matching categories for undivided musical Parts and Audiences, not user-selectable capabilities.

An Audience spanning S1, S2, A1, and A2 is called Treble Voices or SA voices. It is derived from Voice Types across Choirs and does not depend on a User's gender.

This shared vocabulary gives future singer capabilities, musical parts, resource audiences, and reporting a common reference without treating their distinct relationships as Section membership.
